MLE/MAP

MLE/MAP

Learning Objectives

  • Recall probability basics, including but not limited to: discrete and continuous random variables, probability mass functions, probability density functions, events vs. random variables, expectation and variance, joint probability distributions, marginal probabilities, conditional probabilities, independence, conditional independence
  • Describe common probability distributions such as the Beta, Dirichlet, Multinomial, Categorical, Gaussian, Exponential, etc.
  • State the principle of maximum likelihood estimation and explain what it tries to accomplish
  • State the principle of maximum a posteriori estimation and explain why we use it
  • Derive the MLE or MAP parameters of a simple model in closed form

Likelihood Function

  • N samples $D={x^{(i)}}_{i=1}^N$ from i.i.d random variable $X$

    • The likelihood function: multiple of pmf/pdf

  • N samples $D={(x^{(i)},y^{(i)})}_{i=1}^N$ from a pair of random variable $X,Y$

  • Conditional likelihood function

    • Case 1: $Y$ is discrete, $L(\theta) = \prod p(y^{(i)}|x^{(i)}, \theta)$
    • Case 2: $Y$ is continuous, $L(\theta) = \prod f(y^{(i)}|x^{(i)}, \theta)$

  • Joint likelihood function

    • Case 1: $X,Y$ is discrete, $L(\theta) = \prod p(x^{(i)}, y^{(i)}|\theta)$
    • Case 2: $X,Y$ is continuous, $L(\theta) = \prod f(x^{(i)}, y^{(i)}|\theta)$
    • Case 3: $Y$ discrete $X$ continuous, $L(\theta) = \prod f(x^{(i)}|y^{(i)},\alpha)p(y^{(i)}|\beta)$
    • Case 4: $Y$ continuous $X$ discrete, $L(\theta) = \prod p(x^{(i)}|y^{(i)}, \alpha)f(y^{(i)}|\beta)$

Maximum Likelihood Estimator

Principle of MLE: Choose the parameters that maximizes the likelihood of the observed data. $$\theta^{MLE} = \argmax\prod_{i=1}^Np(\mathbf{x}^{(i)}|\theta)$$

  • MLE tries to allocate as much probability mass as possible to the things we observed
  • … at the expense of the things we have not observed
  • Calculated by setting derivative of log-likelihood to zero

Maximum a posteriori (MAP) Estimation

Principle of Maximum a posteriori (MAP) Estimation: Choose the parameters that maximize the posterior of the parameters given the data. $$\theta^{MAP} =\argmax\prod_{i=1}^Np(\theta|\mathbf{x}^{(i)}) = \argmax\prod_{i=1}^N\frac{p(\mathbf{x}^{(i)}|\theta) p(\theta)}{p(\mathbf{x}^{(i)})}= \argmax \prod_{i=1}^Np(\mathbf{x}^{(i)}|\theta)p(\theta)$$

  • More smooth estimator than MLE

Example of MLE/MAP

$x^{(i)}\sim Bernoulli(\phi)$, $\phi\sim Beta(\alpha,\beta)$. Let $N_1 = #(x^{(i)} = 1), N_0 = #(x^{(i)} = 0)$

  • $\phi^{MLE} = \frac{N_1}{N_1+N_0}$
  • $\phi^{MAP} = \frac{N_1’}{N_1’+N_0’} = \frac{N_1+\alpha-1}{N_1+\alpha-1+N_0+\beta-1}$
